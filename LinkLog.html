COLD OPEN
TC 00:00
PROBLEM
Follow-up context was scattered across spreadsheets, notes, paper records — and memory.
Link Workers needed each client's current situation before reaching out; retrieving it fast was nearly impossible.
DIRECTION
How might we help Link Workers maintain long-term client context?
How might history, status, and next steps stay visible before, during, and after each interaction?
CHALLENGE
Structure the workflow without replacing human judgement — consistent enough for reporting, flexible enough for different SALC practices.
SOLUTION
A lightweight follow-up support system: prioritize clients, prepare with context, log interactions, and keep continuity across the whole journey.

HIGHLIGHTS — THE PAYOFF, UP FRONT
TC 01:10
Three of five shipped features, each traceable to the observation that demanded it. The process plays in Acts I–III.
HL 01
The queue answers "who first — and why"
The priority dashboard surfaces overdue and due-this-week follow-ups, contact attempts, follow-up type, and priority level — moving workers from "all clients" to "who needs attention now."
obs.015 — a dashboard should be a workflow entry point, not a database view.
final UI — priority dashboard queues
final UI — show client context filter
HL 02
Context on demand, not by default
The context filter lets workers choose what to see before a call — hobbies, barriers, program attendance, transportation, communication preferences — without forcing every detail onto the screen at once.
obs.014 — a follow-up without context is just a cold call with paperwork.
HL 03
Structure that still keeps the story
The guided logging flow pairs required structured fields with freeform notes, quick tags, and next-step scheduling — reporting consistency without flattening the conversation.
obs.016 — checkboxes keep reports consistent. notes keep people human.
final UI — guided log interaction

RESHOOTS — WHAT TESTING CHANGED
Three findings: testers needed to understand the Link Worker role first; the task needed a scenario, not an instruction; and the context filter's cause-and-effect had to be explicit.
before — generic task script
BEFORE — "Complete the follow-up interaction and save the record." Accurate, and meaningless without the role.
→
after — role-based scenario
AFTER — "You are a Link Worker preparing to call Cora Picard. Review her context, log the call, save the profile." Same task, told as a story with a protagonist.
